Hi there! I am Jiayao Li, an undergraduate student majoring in Computer Science and Technology (Elite Class) at Guohao College, Tongji University, with a GPA of 4.63/5 (equivalent to 92.6/100).

I have been granted eligibility for recommended graduate admission for the 2027 cohort and plan to apply for direct Ph.D. programs. My research interests lie in Embodied AI & Robotics, Physical-World AI & Simulation, and 3D Perception & Spatial Understanding.

I am particularly interested in how embodied agents can acquire high-quality interaction data, generalize under distribution shifts, diagnose failures, and improve through closed-loop feedback in real-world environments.

Selected Projects

Scene Parsing and Geometric Vision — Two-Stream Interactive Joint Learning Framework

Developed a two-stream interactive joint learning framework for scene parsing and geometric vision tasks, enhancing segmentation performance through cross-view geometric information and improving semantic segmentation accuracy on vKITTI2, Cityscapes, and KITTI 2015.

Single-View 3D Occupancy Prediction and Spatial Perception — ViPOcc Framework

Performed monocular 3D occupancy prediction by leveraging depth and semantic-instance priors from visual foundation models, an inverse-depth alignment module, and Semantic-guided Non-overlapping Gaussian Mixture Sampling (SNOG) to improve spatial perception.

Dynamic Robot Navigation — Optimization of 2D LiDAR-Based Pedestrian Detection

Developed a dynamic navigation system integrating perception and decision-making for mobile robot navigation in dynamic environments. The perception module was optimized based on the DR-SPAAM, while the decision-making module adopted TD3 reinforcement learning with a four-stage curriculum learning strategy.

Vision-and-Language Navigation, VLN — Literature Review and Exploratory Study

Conducted a systematic review of recent VLN studies. Summarized key challenges in long-horizon navigation, including cross-modal instruction grounding, memory compression, streaming decision-making, and closed-loop error correction, with a focus on VLM-as-Brain paradigms.
